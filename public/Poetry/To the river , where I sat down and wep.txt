To the river,
where I sat down and wept 
Months before, 
When you were just a mere river 
Flowing across my city
I heard you were sacred 
But didn't had courage or curiosity 
To know how-or-why you were called so 
But one day when my clouds were loaded 
And needed a  Down pour 
You called me 

For few days I just stood right in front of you 
Neither of us would utter a word 
Just absolute silence
I thought,
Why would you call me and stay mute 
Was I a joke or just another prank 
But Then after weeks,
I knew why you weren't asking anything 
As you wanted me to trust you 
To let myself pour down 
And weeks passed by then 
I wept and wept 
You would  console me, 
With the silence in the air 
And melody On my ear drums 
I found peace as I lighten myself 
And then I wanted to do you a favour 
But you wanted nothing in return 
And that was the day 
I knew your sacredness
